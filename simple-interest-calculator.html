Understanding Simple Interest

Definition and Formula

Simple interest is calculated using the formula:
A = P x (1 + r/n)^(n*t)
Where:
A = maturity amount
P = principal amount (investment amount)
r = interest rate (in decimal)
n = compounding frequency (annually, semi-annually, quarterly, or monthly)
t = tenure of the investment (in years)

Key Components: Principal, Rate, and Time

Breaking down the formula, we find three essential components: the principal amount, the interest rate, and the time for which the interest is calculated.
