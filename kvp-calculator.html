What is Kisan Vikas Patra?

Kisan Vikas Patra, often referred to as KVP, is a savings scheme offered by the Indian government to promote financial inclusion among the rural and semi-urban population. It provides a safe and guaranteed return on investment, making it an attractive option for risk-averse investors.

Kisan Vikas Patra Scheme

The Kisan Vikas Patra scheme is designed to encourage long-term savings among individuals. Under this scheme, investors deposit a lump sum amount, and the investment matures after a specific period, with the principal amount doubled. The scheme is available at post offices across the country, making it accessible to a wide range of investors.

Kisan Vikas Patra Interest Rate

Kisan Vikas Patra interest rate are subject to change, with the government periodically revising them. As of 2023, the interest rate for KVP is 7.5%. It's essential to stay updated on the latest interest rates, as they directly impact the returns on your investment.

Benefits of Kisan Vikas Patra

Security and Reliability

One of the key benefits of KVP is its government backing, which ensures the safety of your investment. Unlike market-linked investments, KVP offers a fixed return, providing peace of mind to investors.

Flexibility

Kisan Vikas Patra offers flexibility in terms of the investment amount, with no maximum limit. This makes it suitable for both small and large investors.

Tax Benefits

Investments made in KVP are not eligible for tax deductions under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. However, the interest earned is taxable, making it a tax-efficient investment option.

Documents required for Kisan Vikas Patra

1. Identity Proof : Aadhar card / Passport / Voter ID / Driving license / Government-issued identity card

2. Address Proof : Aadhar card / Passport / Voter ID / Utility bills (electricity, water, or gas) / Ration card / Bank statement / Rent agreement

3. Photographs : You will need passport-sized photographs of yourself.

4. PAN (Permanent Account Number) Card : You may be required to provide your PAN card details for KVP investments. It is mandatory if your investment amount is above a specified limit.

5. Age Proof : If your age is not mentioned on your other documents, you may need to provide a birth certificate or any official document that verifies your age.

6. Nomination Form : You may need to fill out a nomination form, indicating the person who will receive the maturity amount in case of your demise.

7. KYC (Know Your Customer) : You will need to complete the KYC process as per the guidelines set by the issuing bank or post office. This may involve filling out a KYC form and providing relevant documents.

8. Cash or Cheque : You will need to deposit the required investment amount in cash or by check.

The maturity amount of a KVP investment is calculated using the following formula:

Maturity Value = Principal amount * (1 + r/100)n
Where:
Maturity value = the total value of the investment at maturity
Principal amount = the amount invested initially
r = the rate of interest offered by the scheme
n = the number of years the investment is held

How to Invest in Kisan Vikas Patra at Post Office

Investing in KVP is a straightforward process. Visit your nearest post office and fill out the required forms. You will need to provide your KYC documents and the desired investment amount. The post office will issue you a KVP certificate, which serves as proof of your investment.

FAQ - Frequently asked questions

Can I withdraw KVP before maturity?

You can close Kisan Vikas Patra scheme before maturity. The principal along with the interest can be withdrawn. After 2 years and 6 months period (lock-in period) from the date of issuance, premature withdrawal of KVP is possible.

Kisan Vikas Patra double in how many months?

9 years and few months / 115 months : Kisan Vikas Patra doubles the single-time investment in a period of 9-10 years. Like, if you invest KVP for ₹10,000 will return the corpus of ₹20,000 after maturity.

What happens to KVP after death?

Nomination - The purchaser of the certificate, single or joint holder can nominate any person who in the event of the death of the certificate holder shall be entitled to hold the certificate. The maturity amount of the KVP will disbursed to the nominee once scheme matured.

Is Kisan Vikas Patra a safe investment?

Yes, Kisan Vikas Patra is a safe and government-backed savings scheme that offers a guaranteed return on investment.

What is the minimum and maximum investment amount for KVP?

There is no minimum investment amount for KVP, making it accessible to all. However, there is no maximum limit on the investment amount, allowing you to invest according to your financial goals.

Are the interest rates for KVP fixed?

No, the interest rates for Kisan Vikas Patra can change, and it's essential to stay updated on the latest rates before investing.

Is the interest earned on KVP taxable?

Yes, the interest amount earned from Kisan Vikas Patra is taxable. However, it remains a tax-efficient investment option for many individuals.
