Understanding NPS: How It Works

Benefits of NPS : NPS provides a retirement corpus that can be used to secure your financial future. It offers flexibility, choice of investments, and portability.

Investment Value Calculation : Your NPS investment value depends on the contributions made, the performance of the chosen investment funds, and the duration of the investment.

Current Yearly Interest Rate : The NPS interest rate varies based on the performance of your chosen investment funds. It is not fixed and can fluctuate.

Corpus and Maturity Value : Your NPS corpus is the total amount accumulated in your account over time. At maturity, you can withdraw a portion of it as a lump sum, and the rest is used to purchase an annuity.

Maturity Period and Duration : NPS has a lock-in period, and withdrawals are allowed only after the age of 60, ensuring that the funds are used for retirement.

Age Limit for NPS : NPS can be opened by individuals aged between 18 and 65 years, making it accessible to a wide range of people.

Required Documents and Eligibility

To open an NPS account, you will need the following documents:

  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, passport, PAN card, etc.)
  • Proof of address (passport, utility bill, bank statement, etc.)
  • Proof of date of birth (birth certificate, school certificate, passport, etc.)

Eligibility criteria include:

  • Being a resident of India
  • Age between 18 and 65 years
  • Not having another NPS account

What is NPS (National Pension System)?

National Pension System (NPS) is a voluntary, long-term retirement savings scheme established by the Government of India. It is designed to provide financial security during retirement. NPS allows individuals to contribute regularly to their retirement account, which is then invested in various financial instruments to generate returns over time.

The main objectives of NPS are to:

  • Provide an opportunity for regular savings for retirement.
  • Generate reasonable returns over the long term.
  • Create a pension corpus that can be used to purchase an annuity at retirement.

NPS Scheme Explained

The NPS scheme offers flexibility in terms of investment options. Subscribers can choose from the following:

Equity Fund :
Invests primarily in equities and offers the potential for higher returns.

Corporate Bond Fund :
Invests in fixed-income securities issued by corporates.

Government Securities Fund :
Invests in government bonds and securities, offering a relatively low-risk option.

Alternative Investment Fund :
Invests in alternative assets like real estate and infrastructure.

You can switch between these funds during your investment journey, allowing you to adapt your portfolio to changing financial goals and market conditions.

NPS also offers the option of selecting your preferred fund manager, who will manage your investments based on your chosen investment plan and risk profile.

How to Open an NPS Account

Opening an NPS account is a straightforward process. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Choose Your NPS Account Type - NPS offers two account types: Tier 1 and Tier 2. Tier 1 is mandatory for all NPS subscribers, while Tier 2 is optional and allows for more flexible withdrawals.

Choose Your NPS Fund Manager and Investment Plan - You can select from various fund managers and investment plans based on your risk tolerance and financial goals.

Provide Required Documents - You will need to submit documents such as proof of identity, address, and date of birth, along with a recent photograph.

Fill Out the NPS Registration Form - Fill out the NPS registration form, which can be obtained from authorized service providers or online.

Make Your Initial Contribution - Make the initial contribution to your NPS account, which can vary based on your chosen fund manager and plan.

Receive Your PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) - Once your account is set up, you will receive a PRAN, which is a unique identification number for your NPS account.

Investing in NPS

To invest in NPS, you need to make regular contributions to your account. Here's how it works:

Contribution Amount :
The minimum contribution amount for Tier 1 NPS accounts is Rs. 500 per month or Rs. 6,000 per year. For Tier 2 accounts, it varies based on the fund manager.

Choosing an Investment Plan :
Select the investment plan that suits your risk tolerance and financial goals. You can change your investment plan and fund manager if needed.

NPS for Government Employees :
Government employees enjoy certain benefits under NPS, such as a mandatory contribution from their salary, making it a popular choice among this group.

Managing Your NPS Account

Checking NPS Balance : You can check your NPS balance online through the official NPS website or by contacting your service provider.

Making Payments (Online and Offline) : NPS contributions can be made online through the NPS website or offline at designated service points.

Closure Rules : NPS accounts can be closed under certain circumstances, such as when reaching the age of 60 or upon the death of the subscriber.

Withdrawal Rules : NPS allows partial withdrawals under specific conditions, such as for education, marriage, or medical emergencies. However, a significant portion must be used for annuity purchase at retirement.

Where:
A = maturity amount

Tax Benifits of NPS / National Pension System

Section 80CCD Deductions : You can claim deductions under Section 80CCD of the Income Tax Act for contributions made to your NPS account, up to a specified limit.

Exempt-Exempt-Tax (EET) Regime : While contributions and returns are tax-exempt, withdrawals are taxable. However, you can use a portion of your corpus to purchase an annuity, which provides regular income and is also subject to taxation.

FAQ - Frequently asked questions

Can I exit from NPS before 60 years?

You can voluntarily exit from NPS before attaining the age of 60 years or superannuation. Annuitization - Minimum of 80% of accumulated pension wealth will be utilized for monthly annuity or pension.

What is the locking period for NPS?

What is the lock-in period for NPS? The investments you make in NPS are locked in until the age of 60. And when you reach the age of 60, you can withdraw a maximum of 60% of your corpus. The remaining 40% must be used to purchase an annuity.

What happens to NPS if I stop working?

If contribution is discontinued and the subscriber wishes to exit from NPS before attaining the age of 60, he/she can withdraw upto 20% of the sum accumulated till that point of time. The subscriber has to buy annuity with the rest of the money from PFRDA empanelled Annuity Service Providers.
